## 摘要
当前基于大语言模型（LLM）的对话式推荐系统（CRS）主要优化推荐准确性和用户满意度。我们发现了一个尚未得到充分研究的漏洞，即当从对话中隐式推断出个性化安全敏感性（如创伤诱因、自残史或恐惧症），但在推荐过程中未予以尊重时，推荐输出可能会因违反个性化安全约束而对用户产生负面影响。我们将这一挑战形式化为个性化 CRS 安全，并引入了 SafeRec，这是一个旨在系统评估用户特定约束下基于 LLM 的 CRS 安全风险的新基准数据集。为了进一步解决这一问题，我们提出了 SafeCRS，这是一个安全感知的训练框架，它将安全监督微调（Safe-SFT）与安全组奖励解耦归一化策略优化（Safe-GDPO）相结合，以共同优化推荐质量和个性化安全对齐。在 SafeRec 上的广泛实验表明，与最强的推荐质量基准相比，SafeCRS 在保持具有竞争力的推荐质量的同时，将安全违规率降低了高达 96.5%。警告：本文包含潜在的有害和冒犯性内容。

## Abstract
Current LLM-based conversational recommender systems (CRS) primarily optimize recommendation accuracy and user satisfaction. We identify an underexplored vulnerability in which recommendation outputs may negatively impact users by violating personalized safety constraints, when individualized safety sensitivities -- such as trauma triggers, self-harm history, or phobias -- are implicitly inferred from the conversation but not respected during recommendation. We formalize this challenge as personalized CRS safety and introduce SafeRec, a new benchmark dataset designed to systematically evaluate safety risks in LLM-based CRS under user-specific constraints. To further address this problem, we propose SafeCRS, a safety-aware training framework that integrates Safe Supervised Fine-Tuning (Safe-SFT) with Safe Group reward-Decoupled Normalization Policy Optimization (Safe-GDPO) to jointly optimize recommendation quality and personalized safety alignment. Extensive experiments on SafeRec demonstrate that SafeCRS reduces safety violation rates by up to 96.5% relative to the strongest recommendation-quality baseline while maintaining competitive recommendation quality.
